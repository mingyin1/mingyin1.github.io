Research Experiences

Robust Federated Learning Mitigates Client-side Data Reconstruction Attacks
    Supervisor: Prof. Neil Gong, Duke University
  • Find that existing Byzantine-robust aggregation rules could mitigate the client-side inference attacks on FL.
  • Introduce InferGuard, an innovative defense framework designed to protect against inference attacks on the client side of FL. InferGuard effectively mitigates malicious clients’ influence while preserving the FL system’s utility.
  • Propose adaptive attack using PGD and improved the effectiveness of the attack, but InferGuard still tested effective.
Manipulating Federated Recommender Systems with Fake Clients
    Supervisor: Prof. Neil Gong, Duke University
  • Find the limitations of existing attacks against federated recommender systems.
  • Propose a poisoning attack on federated recommender systems that leverages fake users to implement the attack. Those fake users do not require any prior knowledge of genuine users’ information and they do not have local training data.
  • Tested effective when proportion of fake users is extremely low, under which scenario all other existing attacks are ineffective.
